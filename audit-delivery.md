<div class="parchment-section" style="color: #000;">
<h3>1. The IGNIS Audit: Signal vs. Noise</h3>
<p class="audit-subtitle">(The First Law of Influence)</p>
<p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">The first law of influence is: Influence = (Pattern Coherence × Amplitude) / Noise. The Performer shouts louder. The Architect builds a more <em>coherent</em> signal.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Diagnostic 1:</strong> Can every person on your team state your company's mission in <em>one</em> simple, identical sentence? (If not, you have a <strong>Coherence</strong> fracture.)</li>
<li><strong>Diagnostic 2:</strong> Read your last five marketing emails and your last five internal memos. Do they sound like they came from the same universe, or are you polluting your own signal? (This is "Acoustic Warfare.")</li>
<li><strong>Audit Question:</strong> Is your company a pure, coherent <strong>Signal</strong>, or are you just contributing to the <strong>Noise</strong>?</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="parchment-section" style="color: #000;">
<h3>2. The AQUA Audit: The Story Current</h3>
<p class="audit-subtitle">(The Hero's Journey)</p>
<p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">Your marketing is a "Narrative." It either creates "Narrative Transportation" or it is ignored. The greatest failure of Performer marketing is mistaking the brand for the Hero.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Diagnostic 1:</strong> Read your website's "About" page. Is <em>your company</em> the hero ("We are the best, we have 20 years of experience...")?</li>
<li><strong>Diagnostic 2:</strong> Or, is <em>your customer</em> the Hero? Is it clear that <strong>you are the Mentor</strong>, the guide who gives the Hero the plan and tools to win their <em>own</em> story?</li>
<li><strong>Audit Question:</strong> Are you architecting a <strong>Story</strong> for your customer to live in, or just performing a <strong>monologue</strong> at them?</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="parchment-section" style="color: #000;">
<h3>3. The TERRA Audit: The Architecture of Repetition</h3>
<p class="audit-subtitle">(The Habit Loop)</p>
<p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">Your business is not a collection of people; it is a "Cathedral of Continuity" built from systems. These systems are "Brand Rituals" and "Habit Loops" (Cue, Routine, Reward) that either create coherence or chaos.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Diagnostic 1:</strong> Map your sales process. Is it a defined, repeatable <strong>Ritual</strong> that guides a client from Cue (their pain) to Routine (your process) to Reward (their transformation)?</li>
<li><strong>Diagnostic 2:</strong> Do you have a "living scripture" (like your TCTC-Management-System-Blueprint.pdf) that scales your values, or is it just a collection of random documents?</li>
<li><strong>Audit Question:</strong> Is your business an <strong>Architecture of Repetition</strong>, or is it a shack built of reactive, day-to-day decisions?</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="parchment-section" style="color: #000;">
<h3>4. The AER Audit: The Atmosphere of Meaning</h3>
<p class="audit-subtitle">(Language as Climate)</p>
<p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">Your company culture is an "Atmosphere." The language you use is the "air" your team breathes. This "Linguistic Ecosystem" determines what is possible.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Diagnostic 1:</strong> Listen to your team's meetings. Is the language one of <strong>Accountability</strong> and <strong>Curiosity</strong> ("How can we architect a solution?")?</li>
<li><strong>Diagnostic 2:</strong> Or is it a "Hegemony" of blame, jargon, and toxic assumptions ("That's not my department," "We've always done it this way")?</li>
<li><strong>Audit Question:</strong> Have you intentionally designed your <strong>Language as a Climate</strong> for growth, or are you all suffocating in a "smog" of old, unexamined beliefs?</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="parchment-section" style="color: #000;">
<h3>5. The PORTA DOLORIS Audit: The Gravity of the Truth</h3>
<p class="audit-subtitle">( The Pain Gate)</p>
<p style="margin-bottom: 1.5rem;">A problem is not a problem until it is <em>felt</em>. Transformation does not begin until you quantify its "gravity." You must separate the "Presenting Problem" from the "Core Problem."</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Diagnostic 1 (The Symptom):</strong> What is the "Presenting Problem" you complain about? (e.g., "We miss deadlines," "Sales are flat.")</li>
<li><strong>Diagnostic 2 (The Gravity):</strong> What is the true <strong>Mechanical Cost</strong>? (e.g., "We waste 80 man-hours a week on revisions, costing $15,000/month.")</li>
<li><strong>Diagnostic 3 (The Pain Gate):</strong> What is the <strong>Emotional Consequence</strong>? (e.g., "My best people are quitting," "I feel like a failure," "I have no trust in my team.")</li>
<li><strong>Audit Question:</strong> Now that you have passed the <strong>Pain Gate</strong>, what is the <strong>true, unadulterated cost</strong> of <em>not</em> changing your architecture?</li>
</ul>
